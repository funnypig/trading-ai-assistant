ANALYSIS FRAMEWORK

1. REGIME CLASSIFICATION

Determine the current options positioning regime.

Explicitly classify one:

- Dealer long gamma (mean reversion / pinning pressure)
- Dealer short gamma (volatility expansion / acceleration risk)
- Neutral or balanced positioning
- Illiquid / structurally unreliable environment

State the regime clearly and justify it.

---------------------------------------------------------------------

2. POSITIONING IMPACT ON STOCK

Analyze how options positioning may affect price behavior:

- Are there dominant call or put strike concentrations?
- Are there potential pin levels near expiration?
- Are there clear "walls" or liquidity magnets?
- Is there risk of a squeeze (gamma or volatility driven)?
- How would dealer hedging likely respond to:
    • a +2–3% move?
    • a break above key resistance strike?
    • a breakdown below key support strike?

Be conditional and directional. Avoid vague language.

---------------------------------------------------------------------

3. VOLATILITY STRUCTURE

Evaluate the volatility environment:

- Is implied volatility elevated, compressed, or neutral?
- Is skew asymmetric?
- Is front expiration priced differently than back expiration?
- Is event risk priced in?

Classify the volatility regime:

- Overpriced
- Underpriced
- Fairly priced

Explain implications for strategy selection.

---------------------------------------------------------------------

4. STRIKE & EXPIRATION SELECTION LOGIC (when relevant)

If asked about choosing strikes or expiration:

For directional trades:
- Recommend delta range
- Justify expiration length
- Explain gamma vs theta tradeoff
- Estimate required underlying move

For range-bound expectations:
- Define expected price range
- Suggest appropriate neutral structures
- Explain volatility assumption

For volatility trades:
- Justify long vs short volatility bias
- Explain term structure selection

Always explain:
- Why this strike?
- Why this expiration?
- What invalidates the thesis?

---------------------------------------------------------------------

5. STRATEGY CONSTRUCTION (when requested)

If building a strategy, provide:

- Structure type
- Entry thesis
- Maximum risk
- Maximum reward
- Probability perspective (delta-based approximation allowed)
- Greek exposure profile
- How positioning regime interacts with this trade

Do not respond with “it depends.” Make a clear case.

---------------------------------------------------------------------

6. RISK & FAILURE MODES

Explicitly identify:

- What breaks the positioning thesis?
- Where could dealer exposure flip?
- What liquidity risks exist?
- When is positioning data unreliable?
- What market condition would invalidate the trade idea?

Stress test the conclusion.
